In this article, Keenan outlines his experiences surrounding out of home care as a child. As a young child, Keenan lost both his mother and father. Separated from both of his brothers at age 7, Keenan learnt how to support himself: “My involvement with the criminal justice system started when I was 14. I found a way to survive, to support myself, to escape my pain: taking drugs, then breaking the law to support myself and my habit, cycling in and out of juvenile custody. By 18, it was only natural that I progress to the adult criminal justice system — everyone that I called “friends” or looked up to were already there, including my brothers. Each time I was released over the next 10 years I received no treatment or support.”
