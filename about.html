Welcome to Deadly Connections

As an Aboriginal controlled organisation, our roots lie within the communities we work with. With a lifetime of lived experience in Aboriginal communities, we provide the right type of support when needed.

Vision

Deadly Connection’s vision is to create change, promote healing, empower and improve outcomes for First Nations people, families and communities, particularly those affected by the out of home care/justice system. We will achieve this through the provision of Aboriginal-led, innovative, culturally competent solutions that that provide opportunities, fosters connections, drives individual, community, social and systematic change and addresses the social and emotional well-being of First Nations people to disrupt the perpetual cycles of incarceration and child protection interventions.


Mission

Our Mission is to raise awareness and address Aboriginal over-representation across the justice and out of home care system disrupt the inter-generational cycles of disadvantage, oppression, incarceration, addiction, child protection intervention, grief, loss, trauma of First Nations people, families and communities

Who we are

Deadly Connections Community & Justice Services Inc (Deadly Connections) was established in September 2018 as a direct community response to address the over-representation of Aboriginal people, families and communities in both the out of home care (OOHC) and justice system.

Deadly Connections is a specialist, Aboriginal managed and controlled Not For Profit (NFP) charity organisation, disrupting the inter-generational cycle of disadvantage, perpetual trauma, out of home care (OOHC) and justice system involvement of Australia’s First Nations people through the provision of Aboriginal-led, innovative, culturally competent solutions that creates opportunities, fosters connections, drives individual, social and systematic change.

What we do

Our approach seeks to decolonise current structured systems, create opportunities for choice, alleviate poverty, disadvantage and distress associated with the multiple, complex issues/trauma Aboriginal people face, particularly those effected by the justice/OOHC system.

Our service adopts a life course approach, is embedded in culture and healing, embraces our decolonising model and provides First Nations led, innovative, holistic, individualised, intensive, localised solutions that are based on community needs, culturally responsive models and healing centred engagement strategies.

Deadly Connections is built on the foundation of self-determination - that the voices of First Nations people and communities must inform solutions for matters that affect us. Deadly Connections adopts a culturally responsive approach that will empower First Nations people to better shape our own futures, to lead our own development, provide opportunities, empower, inspire and motivate families and communities.

Who we serve

Deadly Connections helps minimise the cycle of disadvantage, perpetual trauma, out of home care (OOHC) and justice system involvement of Australia’s First Nations people.

We Acknowledge and pay respects to the First Nations People of Australia, the land in which we live and work. We recognise the strength,resilience and capacity of Aboriginal people and pay respects to Elders past, present and emerging.
