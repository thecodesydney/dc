Street Smart Project Brief

In response to the growing concern amongst parents and community members about young people (YP) their drug/alcohol misuse, involvement with the justice system and risky behaviours within the Redfern/Waterloo/Glebe areas, Inside Out Aboriginal Justice Consultancy and Street Industries have collaborated to develop, implement and evaluate a community led and owned early intervention, prevention and diversion project.

What?

The primary aim of this project is to provide early intervention, prevention and diversion from the justice system and risky or anti-social behaviours (and the harmful consequences of this behaviour) for all young people (targeting Aboriginal YP) in Redfern/Waterloo/Glebe aged between 10 - 25 years old.

The Street Smart project accesses YP during times of high risk and targets those who may be disconnected from services, family, education/employment and community through the provision of BBQs, sporting and other pro social activities. Workers adopt a harm minimisation approach and utilise the opportunity to explore/address risky or anti-social behaviours and issues identified by the YP.

The Street Smart project adopts a form of outreach and street based youth work that operates without the use of a centre and occurs where YP "are at" both geographically and developmentally’. This project will operate at times of high risk, undertaken in the late afternoons and evenings (outside of business hours) in public places including; train stations, skate parks, public sporting spaces, parks, and on the street in the Redfern/Waterloo/Glebe areas.

Harm Minimisation focuses on minimising the negative effects of substance abuse on YP, their families, peers and communities. During these activities, the youth workers promote pro social behaviour, activities and harm reduction strategies that aim to reduce drug/alcohol related harm through;

  • Drug and alcohol education including the risks associated with substance abuse
  • Developing, delivering and evaluating a range of culturally competent and accessible programs which aim to: strengthen cultural identity and increase cross cultural understanding, increase community connectedness and social cohesion, build resilience in YP, increase positive health outcomes, support YP to be safe, promote physical and mental well-being and develop life skills and coping strategies for vulnerable YP
  • Prevention and health promotion
  • Information and referral.

Innovative strategies will be utilised to engage the YP in discussions and activities around substance abuse and risky behaviours. Staff will work with YP to remove barriers to engagement with external service providers based on the young person’s presenting needs whilst supporting the development of individual resilience and building community cohesiveness amongst YP.

Where?

The Street Smart project will be alternating fortnightly between Redfern/Waterloo and Glebe. We will be targeting ‘hot spots’ where we know YP congregate or ‘hang out’.

When?

The Street Smart project commenced on Saturday 5th of May, 2018 at the Waterloo greens and operates every Saturday from 4pm to 9pm. We have been targeting our services in the Glebe/Broadway area as we are responding to a need, requests from YP and this is currently where the highest demand is.

Who?

This project targets Aboriginal young people (YP) between the ages of 10-24 years old. Although priority will be given to Aboriginal YP, YP from other cultural backgrounds who wish to engage with the project will not be excluded. Given the time that the project will be delivered it is anticipated 13-17 year olds will be our primary cohort. We will particularly focus on those young people who are ‘at risk’ of involvement with the criminal justice system, current involvement with the justice system (on a community based supervision order or bail), substance use and abuse, mental health issues and engaging in risky behaviour. We want to target those YP who are not currently engaging with any programs, services or organisations.

We Acknowledge and pay respects to the First Nations People of Australia, the land in which we live and work. We recognise the strength,resilience and capacity of Aboriginal people and pay respects to Elders past, present and emerging.
